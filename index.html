Leonid
Foundation

Founded in 2022 in New Jersey by Anna and Edward Murov — in the first days after Russia's full-scale invasion. It began with a single Facebook post and a synagogue filled with boxes. Today it is a stable humanitarian, cultural and advocacy platform.

Their focus: children, women, and mothers affected by war in Ukraine. Aid shipments, refugee resettlement, legal support, trauma therapy for children, and advocacy in Washington, D.C.

📍 National Register of Historic Places, 1979

Glen Foerd on the Delaware

Built in 1850 as a summer home by Philadelphia philanthropist Charles Macalester Jr. — a man who advised U.S. Presidents and whose estate hosted heads of state. Expanded in 1903 by Congressman Robert Foerderer into the Italianate-Classical Revival mansion it is today.

18 acres of grounds, sweeping riverfront views, a rose garden, art gallery (Rembrandt, Van Gogh, Monet), and a stained-glass skylight staircase. The only historic mansion on the Delaware Riverfront open to the public.
